title: Approximate Itai-Zehavi Conjecture for Random Graphs
excerpt_separator: <!--more-->

---

The Itai-Zehavi conjecture states that every $k$ vertex-connected graph contains $k$ vertex-independent spanning trees, that is, $k$ spanning trees rooted at a common vertex $r$, such that for every vertex $v$ the paths between $r$ and $v$ in different trees are internally vertex-disjoint.
We show that with high probability the Itai-Zehavi conjecture holds asymptotically for the binomial random graph $G(n,p)$ when $np=\omega(\log n)$ and for the random regular graph $G(n,d)$ when $d=\omega(\log n)$.
This is joint work with Lawrence Hollom, Lyuben Lichev, Julien Portier and Yiting Wang.
